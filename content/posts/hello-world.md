## Why I'm starting this blog

I've been working in software engineering for over 7 years. Over that time, I've learned a lot of good approaches and practices that really work. A lots of people often ask me: "How do I start with automation testing?" "How do I write stable tests?" "How do I create good skills and instructions for LLM models?" This blog is where I can answer these questions and help the people around me.

But there's another reason, technology changes so fast. Every day there's a new tool, a new update, a new AI model. I don't always remember the details. How do I structure a different files? How do I create skills for LLM models that the model will actually follow? By writing about these things here, I'm creating a personal notebook that I can come back to whenever I need it. And by sharing it publicly, maybe it helps other people too.

## What to expect

I plan to write about:

- **Test automation** — Playwright, TypeScript, patterns and anti-patterns
- **AI & tooling** — Using LLMs to improve engineering workflows
- **Things I've learned** — Short notes from day-to-day work

Stay tuned. More posts coming soon.
